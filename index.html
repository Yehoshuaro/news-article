Mass Surveillance: How Technology Controls Citizens

By Anna Kovalenko | Published: November 14, 2025, 08:30 AM

In recent years, surveillance technology has become an inescapable part of daily life. Cameras on every corner, smart devices in homes, and algorithms tracking your every move online have created a world where privacy is little more than a myth. A new investigation reveals that governments and corporations are using citizen data for control on an unprecedented scale.

According to a report by an independent group of researchers, over 80% of internet-connected devices collect data without explicit user consent. This includes not only smartphones and computers but also household appliances like smart refrigerators and TVs. Your conversations may be recorded, your movements tracked, and your habits analyzed to build a digital profile.

Experts warn that if this trend continues, individual freedom could be at risk. “We’re moving toward a panopticon society,” said Dr. Elena Volkov, a cybersecurity analyst. “Every breath you take, every move you make—someone, or something, is watching you.”
